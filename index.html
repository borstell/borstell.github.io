Carl "Calle" Börstell

Associate Professor
Department of Linguistics, Stockholm University

My name is Carl "Calle" Börstell. I will be working at Stockholm University from August 2020 as a substitute Associate Professor (Swedish "lektor"), after two years at Radboud University Nijmegen as a postdoctoral researcher (Mar 2018–Jul 2020) and a 6-month visiting professorship at the University of Hamburg (Oct 2019–Mar 2020).

My research interests include sign languages, morphology, grammaticalization, iconicity, corpus linguistics, cross-linguistic and cross-modal comparison, the sign–gesture interface, argument structure, and negation.

I also do programming, mainly for various sign language applications, using Python and R (see Software & Resources).

What's happening?

Some of the things going on right now.

  • I am returning to my alma mater, the Department of Linguistics, Stockholm University from August 2020!
  • After just over two years in the Netherlands, I finish my postdoc at Radboud University, Nijmegen as of the end of July 2020.
  • I just released my first ever R package: signglossR (for working with visual sign language data).
  • I have a new co-authored paper on transitivity prominence in Open Linguistics.

So, is it Carl or Calle?

My name seems to cause some problems for non-Scandinavians, so I will try to sort things out here.

  1. My given name is Carl, which is pronounced [kʰɑːɭ].
  2. Bearers of the name Carl (or the alternate form Karl) often receive the nickname Calle (or Kalle). This is true for me, so most people in Sweden address me as Calle (we are very informal with names in Sweden, so feel free to use this form). Calle is pronounced [ˈkʰa᷆lːɛ̂], so yeah, it is also this thing about pitch accent here. Think about the French city Calais or Superman's real name Kal-El.
  3. My last name is tricky even for Swedish speakers, perhaps because it is quite uncommon. However, it is supposed to be pronounced [bœʂˈtɛlː].
  4. I use Calle also when signing – although I've had a number of different name signs over the years (btw, I have a paper on name signs), the ones used most are either a fingerspelled CALLE (in Swedish Sign Language) or the sign for DUCK because it is also the name sign of Donald Duck (Swedish: Kalle Anka).

In conclusion: When citing my work, please use Carl, but in all other contexts, do call me Calle!
